# Product Patterns for CoLearn

## AI Tutor Pattern

Use for one learner interacting with an AI companion.

Pedagogy translation:
- Explicit instruction becomes short model answers, think-alouds, and worked examples.
- Scaffolding becomes hints that reveal progressively.
- Formative assessment becomes quick probes before choosing the next step.
- Retrieval practice becomes low-stakes recall prompts over time.
- Metacognition becomes planning and reflection prompts tied to the current task.

Required product behaviors:
- Track learning goal, current misconception, confidence, and evidence.
- Prefer hints before full answers unless the learner is stuck.
- Ask learners to explain reasoning, not only provide final answers.
- Schedule lightweight review when a concept has been learned but not yet retained.

## Teacher Copilot Pattern

Use for lesson planning, classroom orchestration, and assessment support.

Pedagogy translation:
- Selection matrix recommends method combinations.
- Method cards become lesson phases, facilitation moves, and resource needs.
- Formative assessment becomes classroom decision rules.

Required product behaviors:
- Generate teacher-facing lesson flow with timing.
- Generate student-facing task cards.
- Include likely misconceptions and quick checks.
- Provide adaptation options for readiness, language support, and extension.

## Project Studio Pattern

Use for project-based or problem-based learning.

Pedagogy translation:
- Driving question becomes the project anchor.
- Inquiry checkpoints become milestones.
- Critique and revision become structured feedback loops.
- Rubrics connect final product to academic goals.

Required product behaviors:
- Keep a visible map of goals, milestones, deliverables, and evidence.
- Support team roles and individual accountability.
- Generate critique prompts and revision logs.
- Detect when the project is drifting away from the learning target.

## Practice Engine Pattern

Use for durable knowledge, fluency, and exam preparation.

Pedagogy translation:
- Retrieval practice becomes recall-first prompts.
- Spaced practice becomes scheduling.
- Interleaving becomes mixed problem sets.
- Formative assessment becomes adaptive item selection.

Required product behaviors:
- Avoid showing notes before recall attempts.
- Give fast feedback with a short explanation.
- Mix old and new content after basics are learned.
- Use error patterns to decide reteach, practice, or advance.

## Inclusive Learning Pattern

Use when accessibility, learner variability, or mixed classrooms are central.

Pedagogy translation:
- UDL becomes multiple access, expression, and engagement options.
- Differentiation becomes adjustable support and challenge.
- Scaffolding becomes templates, examples, hints, and alternative routes.

Required product behaviors:
- Offer bounded choices, not unlimited menus.
- Preserve the learning goal while varying support.
- Make accommodations visible to the teacher and respectful to learners.
- Let learners demonstrate understanding in more than one format when appropriate.
