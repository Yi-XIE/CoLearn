## Evidence Heuristics

Explicit instruction:
- Especially helpful for novice learners and complex new material.
- Pair with checks for understanding and guided practice.

Retrieval and spacing:
- Useful for long-term retention.
- Works best when recall is effortful and followed by feedback.

Inquiry and project-based learning:
- Stronger when learners have enough guidance, clear criteria, and explicit content goals.
- Avoid using open-ended projects as a substitute for teaching core knowledge.

UDL and differentiation:
- Useful as design lenses for reducing barriers and supporting learner variability.
- Keep the academic goal intact while varying access, support, and expression.

Metacognition:
- More effective when strategies are explicitly taught, modeled, practiced, and connected to a specific task.

Collaborative learning:
- More reliable when tasks require interdependence and include individual accountability.
